Shayara Bano v. Union of India

Understanding a 200+ Page Supreme Court Judgment in 10 Minutes

The Story Behind the Case

Shayara Bano was married in 2002. In October 2015, her husband declared “Triple Talaq” through a letter, instantly ending their marriage.

This form of divorce required no court process, no justification, and no opportunity for reconciliation. The marriage ended immediately.

Believing this violated her fundamental rights, she approached the Supreme Court of India in 2016.

What is Triple Talaq?

Triple Talaq (Talaq-e-Biddat) allowed a Muslim man to divorce his wife instantly by saying “talaq” three times.

  • No judicial oversight
  • No mandatory reason
  • No reconciliation period
  • Immediate and irreversible

The Constitutional Conflict

The Supreme Court had to decide whether a religious practice could override fundamental rights such as equality and dignity.

The key legal question was: Does Triple Talaq violate Article 14 (Right to Equality)?
